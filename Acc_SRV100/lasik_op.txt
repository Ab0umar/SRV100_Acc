Scope
- SQL Server database inspected: op2026
- OP files inspected under: D:\OP
- Focus only on Section 15 (LASIK), patient queries, doctor queries, receipt queries, and service revenue reports

1. Section 15 Identity

- Live SQL Server check confirms:
  - Table: dbo.DEPT
  - DPT_NO = 15
  - DPT_NM_AR = اللــــــيزك

2. Tables Actually In Use

- dbo.DEPT
  - Section lookup table
  - Used to resolve Section 15 name and section filtering

- dbo.SRVCMF
  - LASIK service master
  - LASIK services are stored with DPT_NO = 15

- dbo.MDTEAM
  - Doctor master
  - LASIK doctors are stored with DPT_NO = 15

- dbo.PAPATMF
  - Patient master
  - Used for PAT_CD and patient name lookups

- dbo.PAJRNRCVH
  - Receipt/header table
  - Used for sec_cd, pat_cd, tr_no, tr_dt, tr_ty, totl, enteredby

- dbo.PAPAT_SRV
  - Service-line table
  - Used for sec_cd, pat_cd, srv_cd, srv_by1, qty, prc, pa_vl, ca_vl, disc_vl

- dbo.PAPAT_PAY
  - Used by payment/refund/insurance receipt-style reports

3. What Is Actually LASIK-Specific

- LASIK is not implemented as a separate special database structure
- The LASIK slice is the generic OP accounting structure filtered by:
  - DEPT.DPT_NO = 15
  - SRVCMF.DPT_NO = 15
  - MDTEAM.DPT_NO = 15
  - transaction/service rows where SEC_CD = 15
